## Target Segment

**Who:** Car enthusiasts in Bulgaria, aged 20-40
**Profile:** Owns one or more vehicles (typically used European — BMW, Audi, VW, Mercedes). Actively maintains, modifies, or discusses their car. Active in online car communities (Facebook groups, forums, Instagram). Spends €200-500/month on their car. Currently has no clear picture of total ownership costs.

**Goal:** Validate whether Bulgarian car enthusiasts (a) want to understand their true car ownership costs, (b) would actually track expenses in an app, and (c) would pay for deeper insights. Secondarily, understand their current behavior, pain points, and relationship with existing tools (Fuelio, Drivvo).

**Methodology:** The Mom Test — talk about their life, not our idea.

---

## Interview Setup

**Duration:** 15-20 minutes
**Format:** In person (car meets, garage visits), phone, or video call
**Target count:** 15-20 interviews minimum
**Recording:** Ask permission to take notes. Don't record unless they explicitly agree.

**Golden rules:**
- Do NOT pitch the app idea until the very end (if at all)
- Ask open-ended questions, then be quiet
- Follow up with "why?" and "tell me more about that"
- Pay attention to emotions — frustration, surprise, excitement
- If they say something interesting, dig deeper: "Can you give me a specific example?"
- Don't fill silences. Let them think.

**Where to recruit:**
- Bulgarian car Facebook groups (BMW Club Bulgaria, Audi Club BG, VW community, etc.)
- Car meets and events
- Auto repair garage waiting rooms
- Car wash locations
- Instagram DMs to active car accounts
- Friends and friends-of-friends who are car people

**Recruitment message (example):**
"Hey! I'm researching how car owners in Bulgaria manage their car expenses. I'd love to chat for 15 minutes about your experience — no selling, just conversation. Would you be open to a quick call this week?"

## Questions

### Section 1: Warm-Up (2 min)

Purpose: Get them talking about their car. Build rapport.

**Q1:** "Tell me about your car — what do you drive?"
- *Follow-up:* "How long have you had it? What do you like most about it?"

**Q2:** "What's the last thing you did to your car — maintenance, modification, anything?"
- *Follow-up:* "How much did that cost? Where did you get it done?"

*Notes for interviewer: Let them talk. Enthusiasts love talking about their car. This builds trust and reveals their level of involvement.*

---

### Section 2: Current Behavior — How They Track Today (5 min)

Purpose: Understand what they actually DO today. Past behavior predicts future behavior.

**Q3:** "Do you have any idea roughly how much you spend on your car per month? Or per year?"
- *Wait for answer. Don't suggest a number.*
- *Follow-up:* "How did you come up with that number?"

**Q4:** "Do you keep track of what you spend on your car in any way? Notebook, spreadsheet, app, anything?"
- *If yes:* "Walk me through how you do it. When did you start? How often do you update it?"
- *If no:* "Has it ever crossed your mind to track it? Why/why not?"

**Q5:** "When was the last time a car expense surprised you — something that cost more than you expected?"
- *Follow-up:* "What happened? How did you handle it?"

**Q6:** "When you need to make a decision about your car — like whether to fix something or replace a part — how do you decide? Where do you get information?"
- *Listen for:* Facebook groups, friends, mechanic's advice, Google, gut feeling

*Notes for interviewer: This section reveals whether they have any tracking behavior at all. Most won't. That's the baseline. Pay attention to HOW they estimate costs — gut feeling vs. actual data.*

---

### Section 3: Pain Discovery — The Real Frustrations (5 min)

Purpose: Find genuine pain points. These must be real, not hypothetical.

**Q7:** "What's the most frustrating thing about owning a car in Bulgaria, cost-wise?"
- *Let them vent. Don't steer.*

**Q8:** "Have you ever been in a situation where you wished you had a complete history of everything you've spent on your car?"
- *If yes:* "Tell me about that situation. What happened?"
- *If no:* "That's fine — not everyone needs that."

**Q9:** "Have you ever wondered how your car costs compare to other people with the same model? Like, whether you're spending more or less than average?"
- *Follow-up:* "What would you do with that information if you had it?"

**Q10:** "Do you ever document your car — take photos of modifications, keep records of work done?"
- *If yes:* "Where do you keep that? How do you organize it?"
- *If no:* "Is there a reason you don't?"

*Notes for interviewer: Q7 is the most important question. Listen for emotional intensity. "Yeah it's annoying" = low signal. "It drives me crazy that I have no idea where my money goes" = high signal.*

---

### Section 4: Competitor Probing (3 min)

Purpose: Understand awareness and experience with existing tools.

**Q11:** "Have you ever tried any app for tracking car expenses? Like Fuelio, Drivvo, or anything similar?"

- *If yes:*
  - "What made you try it?"
  - "What did you like about it?"
  - "Why did you stop using it?" *(if they stopped)*
  - "What was missing?"

- *If no:*
  - "Have you heard of any apps like that?"
  - "Is there a reason you haven't tried one?"

**Q12:** "If you think about the apps you pay for on your phone — Spotify, Netflix, anything — what makes you willing to pay for those?"
- *Follow-up:* "How much do you typically spend on apps per month?"

*Notes for interviewer: Competitor experience reveals a lot. If they tried Fuelio and stopped, the "why" is gold. If they never tried anything, probe why — is it apathy, lack of awareness, or distrust of apps?*

---

### Section 5: Value Testing (3 min)

Purpose: Gently test whether our core value propositions resonate. Still no pitching.

**Q13:** "Imagine you could open your phone and instantly see: 'Your car costs you exactly X leva per month, and X stotinki per kilometer.' Would that change anything about how you think about your car?"
- *Follow-up:* "What would you do with that number?"

**Q14:** "What if you could see how your costs compare to other owners of the same car model — like 'Your BMW E46 costs 15% more per kilometer than average.' Would that be interesting to you?"
- *Follow-up:* "Would you trust that data?"

**Q15:** "If there was one thing an app could do to genuinely help you with car ownership — not just tracking, but actually helping — what would it be?"
- *This is an open-ended dream question. Listen carefully.*

*Notes for interviewer: Q13 tests the "aha moment" hypothesis. Watch their face/voice when you say the exact number. Surprise or curiosity = strong signal. Shrug = weak signal. Q15 often reveals things you haven't thought of.*

---

### Section 6: Commitment & Close (2 min)

Purpose: Test real interest through action, not words.

**Q16:** "We're working on something in this space. It's early. Would you be willing to try an early version and give us honest feedback when it's ready?"
- *If yes:* "Great — can I get your email or phone number to reach out?"
- *If no:* That's valuable data too. Don't push.

**Q17:** "Do you know 2-3 other car people who might be interested in talking about this? I'd love to hear their perspective too."
- *If they offer introductions, that's a strong commitment signal.*

**Q18:** "Is there anything I should have asked about that I didn't? Anything about car ownership that frustrates you that we didn't cover?"
- *Sometimes the best insights come from this question.*

*Notes for interviewer: Email/phone signup = real interest. Introductions = even stronger. "Yeah sounds cool" with no action = politeness, not passion.*

## After Each Interview

### Signal Scoring (rate 1-5)

| Signal | Score | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Problem awareness** — Do they recognize that they don't know their costs? | /5 | |
| **Current behavior** — Are they actively trying to solve this (even poorly)? | /5 | |
| **Emotional intensity** — Did they express frustration, surprise, or excitement? | /5 | |
| **Willingness to act** — Would they try a solution? Pay? Refer others? | /5 | |
| **Frequency of need** — How often do cost-related situations come up? | /5 | |
| **TOTAL** | /25 | |

**Scoring guide:**
- 20-25: Strong validation signal. This person IS our target user.
- 15-19: Moderate signal. Interested but may not be a power user.
- 10-14: Weak signal. Problem exists but not painful enough to drive action.
- Below 10: Not our user. Move on.

### Key Quotes
- Write down their exact words. These become marketing copy later.
- Especially capture: moments of surprise, frustration, or "I wish..."

### Surprises
- Note anything that challenges our assumptions
- Note any feature ideas or needs we hadn't considered

## Red Flags (Weak Validation)

- "Yeah, that sounds cool" — polite, not passionate
- Can't describe a specific time costs frustrated them — low pain
- "I'd use it if it was free" — no willingness to pay
- Never tried to solve the problem in any way — low motivation
- "I just ask my mechanic" — may not need a tool
- Talks about what they WOULD do, never what they HAVE done — future vs. past

## Green Lights (Strong Validation)

- Describes workarounds: spreadsheets, notes app, mental math, asking Facebook groups
- Emotional reaction when they realize they don't know their costs: "Honestly? I have no idea. That's kind of embarrassing."
- Asks when they can try it — unprompted interest
- Offers to refer friends without being asked
- Mentions specific amounts that surprised them: "The timing belt cost me 1,200 leva. I was NOT expecting that."
- Currently uses Fuelio/Drivvo but complains about limitations
- Has ever sold a car and wished they had a cost history

## Analysis Plan

After completing 15-20 interviews:

1. **Tally signal scores** — Average across all interviews. If mean > 17, strong validation.
2. **Pattern analysis** — What pain points came up most? What surprised us?
3. **Segment identification** — Did different types of enthusiasts emerge? (DIY vs. garage-reliant, single-car vs. multi-car, tracking-inclined vs. tracking-averse)
4. **Quote bank** — Compile the best quotes for positioning and marketing copy.
5. **Assumption validation** — Which of our assumptions from the concept document were confirmed or refuted?
6. **Feature priority signals** — Which features resonated most? Cost dashboard? Timeline? Benchmarks?

## Assumptions (Pre-Interview)

These are what we believe BEFORE talking to customers. Interviews should validate or refute each one.

| # | Assumption | Confidence | Interview Questions That Test It |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Car enthusiasts don't know their true monthly car cost | High | Q3, Q5 |
| 2 | They want to know (problem awareness exists) | Medium | Q7, Q8, Q13 |
| 3 | They're not currently tracking in any meaningful way | High | Q4 |
| 4 | A precise cost/km number would be an "aha moment" | Medium | Q13 |
| 5 | Comparison with other owners is compelling | Medium | Q9, Q14 |
| 6 | They'd actually log expenses in an app (behavior change) | Low | Q4, Q11, Q16 |
| 7 | They'd pay €2.99/month for premium insights | Low | Q12 |
| 8 | Vehicle timeline/photo documentation appeals to enthusiasts | Medium | Q10 |
