## 1. Customer Profile

### Target: Car enthusiasts in Bulgaria, aged 20-40

---

### 1.1 Customer Jobs

What our target user is trying to accomplish in their life related to car ownership.

#### Functional Jobs

| # | Job | Frequency | Importance |
|---|---|---|---|
| F1 | Maintain and repair their car to keep it running well | Ongoing (monthly+) | Critical |
| F2 | Pay for fuel efficiently (find good prices, track consumption) | Weekly | High |
| F3 | Budget for car-related expenses (insurance, tax, tires, maintenance) | Monthly/annually | High |
| F4 | Make informed decisions about repairs (fix vs. replace, mechanic vs. DIY) | As-needed | High |
| F5 | Keep records for when they sell the car (prove maintenance history) | Long-term | Medium |
| F6 | Manage multiple vehicles (many enthusiasts own 2-3 cars) | Ongoing | Medium |
| F7 | Stay on top of maintenance schedules (oil change, timing belt, tires) | Periodic | High |
| F8 | Handle administrative tasks (insurance renewal, annual tax, inspection) | Annual | Medium |

#### Emotional Jobs

| # | Job | Context |
|---|---|---|
| E1 | Feel in control of a major expense | "I know where my money goes" |
| E2 | Feel smart about car ownership decisions | "I make data-driven choices, not gut feelings" |
| E3 | Feel proud of their car and what they've built | "Look at what I've done to this car" |
| E4 | Feel validated that their car spending is reasonable | "I'm not crazy — others spend this much too" |
| E5 | Feel connected to the car community | "I'm part of something" |

#### Social Jobs

| # | Job | Context |
|---|---|---|
| S1 | Show their car to others (social media, car meets) | "Check out my build" |
| S2 | Compare experiences with other owners | "Is your E46 this expensive too?" |
| S3 | Get respect for knowledge and commitment | "He knows everything about his car" |
| S4 | Defend car spending to family/partners | "Here are the actual numbers — it's not as bad as you think" |

---

### 1.2 Pains

What frustrates, annoys, or blocks our target user.

| # | Pain | Severity | Current Workaround |
|---|---|---|---|
| P1 | **Don't know their true monthly/yearly car cost** — expenses are scattered and forgotten | High | Mental math (inaccurate), do nothing |
| P2 | **Surprised by unexpected costs** — big repair bills, insurance increases, accumulated small expenses | High | Absorb the shock, complain to friends |
| P3 | **No organized history** — can't remember what was done, when, or how much it cost | High | Receipts in a box, memory, ask the mechanic |
| P4 | **Can't compare costs** — no way to know if their spending is normal or if they're overpaying | Medium | Ask Facebook groups (unreliable anecdotes) |
| P5 | **Forget maintenance deadlines** — miss oil changes, tire rotations, inspection dates | Medium | Memory, sticky notes, random calendar reminders |
| P6 | **Existing apps are boring** — Fuelio/Drivvo feel like accounting software, not car apps | Medium | Stop using the app after 2 weeks |
| P7 | **No proof of vehicle history when selling** — can't demonstrate maintenance to buyers | Medium | Verbal claims, scattered receipts |
| P8 | **Tedious manual entry** — logging expenses feels like work, especially consistently | High | Give up tracking entirely |
| P9 | **Family/partner pressure** about car spending with no data to show | Medium | Defensive arguments, vague estimates |
| P10 | **Information overload from Facebook groups** — conflicting advice, no data backing | Low | Trust the loudest voice or most-liked comment |

**Ranking by impact on product-market fit:**
1. P1 + P2 (cost blindness) — Existential. If these aren't real pains, the product has no reason to exist.
2. P8 (tedious entry) — The retention killer. Even if the pain is real, friction in the solution kills adoption.
3. P3 + P7 (no history) — Strong secondary driver, especially for the vehicle timeline feature.
4. P4 (can't compare) — The benchmarking hook, but requires critical mass of users.
5. P6 (boring apps) — Design opportunity, not a standalone pain.

### 2.2 Pain Relievers

| Feature/Capability | Pain Relieved | How |
|---|---|---|
| **Automatic cost aggregation** into single dashboard | P1: Don't know true cost | No mental math needed — the app calculates total monthly cost, yearly cost, and cost/km automatically |
| **Category breakdown with trends** | P2: Surprised by costs | See where money goes. Spot rising categories. No more "where did all that money go?" |
| **Vehicle timeline** with dates, costs, photos, notes | P3: No organized history | Every expense, service, and modification is recorded chronologically. Always accessible. |
| **Model benchmarks** ("Your car costs X% more/less than average") | P4: Can't compare costs | Real data from other owners replaces Facebook group anecdotes |
| **Smart reminders** (mileage + date triggers) | P5: Forget maintenance | Proactive alerts before deadlines — oil change in 500 km, insurance renewal in 30 days |
| **Car culture design** — vehicle timeline, photo documentation, enthusiast-friendly UI | P6: Boring apps | The app feels like a car app, not accounting software. Celebrating ownership, not just tracking it. |
| **Exportable service history** (PDF) | P7: No proof when selling | Generate a professional document showing every service and expense — instant credibility for buyers |
| **Quick-add expense entry** — minimal taps, smart defaults, recent categories | P8: Tedious entry | Designed for 10-second logging. Reduce friction to the absolute minimum to protect the habit. |
| **Sharable dashboard/cost summary** | P9: Family pressure | "Here are the actual numbers" — data replaces arguments |
| **Aggregated data-backed insights** (vs. anecdotes) | P10: Information overload | Real benchmarks based on thousands of data points, not one person's opinion in a Facebook comment |

## 3. Fit Analysis

### How well does the value map address the customer profile?

| Customer Need | Product Answer | Fit Level | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **P1: Don't know true cost** | Cost dashboard (monthly total, cost/km) | STRONG | Core feature. Direct answer to #1 pain. |
| **P2: Surprised by costs** | Category trends + spending forecasts | STRONG | Free tier shows breakdown. Premium adds predictions. |
| **P3: No organized history** | Vehicle timeline | STRONG | Unique feature. No competitor offers this well. |
| **P4: Can't compare** | Model benchmarks | MEDIUM | Requires user data critical mass. Seed with public data. |
| **P5: Forget maintenance** | Smart reminders | STRONG | Table stakes — competitors do this. We must match. |
| **P6: Boring apps** | Car culture design | STRONG | Design differentiation. Hard to measure but real. |
| **P7: No proof when selling** | PDF export | STRONG | Premium feature. Clear value for sellers. |
| **P8: Tedious entry** | Quick-add UX | CRITICAL | Existential. If entry is painful, nothing else matters. |
| **P9: Family pressure** | Sharable dashboard | MEDIUM | Nice-to-have. Not a primary conversion driver. |
| **P10: Info overload** | Data-backed benchmarks | MEDIUM | Requires scale. Long-term differentiator. |

### Fit Summary

**Strong fit areas (3):**
1. Cost visibility (P1 + P2 → dashboard + trends)
2. Vehicle history (P3 + P7 → timeline + export)
3. Maintenance management (P5 → reminders)

**Critical risk area (1):**
- P8 (tedious entry) — If we don't nail quick-add UX, the value proposition collapses. Users must be able to log an expense in <10 seconds.

**Growth fit areas (2):**
- Benchmarking (P4 → model comparisons) — gets stronger over time
- Community engagement (P6 + G9 → gamification) — drives retention

## 4. Value Proposition Statement

### Synthesized from the canvas:

> **For car enthusiasts in Bulgaria** who spend €200-500/month on their vehicles but have no idea what their car truly costs them,
>
> **our platform** is a car ownership intelligence app that **instantly shows your real cost per month and per kilometer, documents your car's complete journey, and tells you how you compare to other owners** —
>
> **unlike Fuelio or Drivvo**, which are basic expense loggers with no insights, no community, and no car culture.
>
> **We know it works when** users say "I had no idea my car cost me that much" and come back every week to log expenses.

## 6. Assumptions to Validate
