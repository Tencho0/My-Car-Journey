## Lean Canvas

### 1. Problem

**Top 3 problems for our target user (car enthusiasts in Bulgaria, aged 20-40):**

1. **No visibility into true car costs.** They spend €200-500/month on their car but can't tell you the total. Expenses are fragmented across fuel, maintenance, mods, insurance, tax, tires, parking, and fines. The real number is always higher than they think.

2. **No organized record of their car's history.** Receipts pile up, memories fade. When they sell or want to review what they've done to the car, there's no single source of truth. Service history exists only in the mechanic's memory.

3. **No way to know if their costs are normal.** "Am I spending too much?" is unanswerable without data from other owners of the same car model. They rely on anecdotes from Facebook groups and friends.

**Existing alternatives (how they solve it today):**
- Do nothing (most common) — complete blindness to real costs
- Mental math — wildly inaccurate, forgets 50%+ of expenses
- Spreadsheet or notes app — tedious, abandoned after 2 weeks
- Fuelio / Drivvo — basic loggers with no insights, no community, no car culture
- Ask Facebook groups — unreliable, anecdotal, answers disappear

*Evidence: Competitor analysis shows no app provides total ownership cost intelligence. Facebook group behavior confirms information-seeking around costs. To be validated through customer interviews.*

### 2. Customer Segments

**Primary (MVP target):**
Car enthusiasts in Bulgaria, aged 20-40

- ~150,000-250,000 people (estimated from Facebook group membership and community activity)
- Owns 1-3 vehicles, typically used European (BMW, Audi, VW, Mercedes)
- Actively maintains, modifies, and discusses their car
- Spends €200-500/month on car-related expenses
- Active in online car communities
- Technically comfortable (smartphone-native)

**Early adopter profile:**
A 25-35 year old male who owns a used BMW E46/E90, is active in BMW Club Bulgaria on Facebook, does some maintenance himself, visits a trusted mechanic for bigger jobs, spends ~350 лв/month on his car, and has no idea that's 4,200 лв/year.

**Secondary segments (Phase 2+):**
- Cost-conscious everyday drivers who want to budget
- Gig economy drivers (Bolt, Glovo, taxi) needing cost tracking for tax purposes
- Auto repair garages wanting digital customer connection
- Used car dealerships
- Small fleet operators

*Segment sizing: See `/01-discovery/research/market-sizing.md`*

### 3. Unique Value Proposition

**Single clear message:**

> "Finally know what your car costs. Not a guess. Not a feeling. The actual number."

**Supporting value:**
- Track every expense across all categories in seconds
- See your total cost per month, per year, and per kilometer
- Compare your costs to other owners of the same car model
- Document your car's journey with a visual timeline
- Compete with friends on cost efficiency and fuel economy

**Why this UVP:**
It addresses the #1 problem directly (not knowing costs) with a clear, emotional promise (clarity replaces uncertainty). It doesn't try to be everything — it leads with the insight, not the features.

*Positioning context: See `/02-strategy/positioning-strategy.md` — Variation D*

### 4. Solution

**Core solution mapped to problems:**

| Problem | Solution | MVP Feature |
|---|---|---|
| No visibility into true costs | Automated cost aggregation and analytics | Cost dashboard: monthly total, cost/km, category breakdown, trends |
| No organized car history | Chronological visual record of everything | Vehicle timeline with photos, notes, service records |
| Can't compare costs | Anonymized benchmarking against same-model owners | Model benchmarks: "Your car costs X% more/less than average" |

**Additional solution components (MVP):**
- Multi-vehicle support (enthusiasts often own multiple cars)
- Maintenance reminders (mileage-based and date-based)
- Quick-add expense entry (minimize friction to maintain the habit)
- Basic gamification: monthly challenges, leaderboards (engagement + retention)
- Shareable vehicle card (social proof + organic growth)

**Explicitly NOT in MVP:**
Offline sync, receipt OCR, bank integration, income tracking, garage/dealer/fleet modules, social profiles, advanced predictions, data intelligence reports.

*Feature details: See `/03-product/mvp-feature-list.md`*

### 5. Channels

**How we reach car enthusiasts in Bulgaria:**

| Channel | Type | Priority | Expected Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Bulgarian car Facebook groups** | Organic community | P0 — Primary | Highest. 300K+ members across groups. Direct access to target. |
| **Car meets and events** | In-person | P0 — Primary | High. Demo the app, get installs, build word-of-mouth. |
| **Instagram car accounts** | Organic social | P1 — Secondary | Medium. Visual platform suits vehicle timeline feature. |
| **Auto.bg forum** | Community | P1 — Secondary | Medium. 30-50K active users, niche but engaged. |
| **App Store / Google Play ASO** | Search | P1 — Secondary | Medium. "Car expense tracker" keywords for discovery. |
| **YouTube (car content creators)** | Influencer | P2 — Future | Medium. Partner with Bulgarian car YouTubers for reviews. |
| **Paid social ads (Facebook/Instagram)** | Paid | P2 — Future | Low budget (€200-500/month). Test after organic channels proven. |

**Channel strategy:**
- Phase 1 (Month 1-3): Organic only. Facebook groups, car meets, personal network.
- Phase 2 (Month 3-6): Add ASO optimization and Instagram presence.
- Phase 3 (Month 6+): Selective paid ads and influencer partnerships.

**Key insight from market sizing:** The target segment is concentrated in identifiable communities. This is an advantage — distribution is not a spray-and-pray problem. We can go directly to where they are.

### 6. Revenue Streams

**Phase 1 (MVP): Premium subscriptions only**

| Plan | Price | Annual Equivalent |
|---|---|---|
| Monthly | €2.99/month (~5.85 лв) | €35.88/year |
| Annual | €24.99/year (~48.85 лв) | €24.99/year (30% discount) |

**No ads.** The app should feel premium. Ads undermine the "intelligence platform" positioning.

**Free tier:** Full expense tracking, basic dashboard (monthly total, category breakdown), 2 vehicles, 5 photos, 3 reminders.

**Premium tier:** Cost per kilometer, advanced analytics, model benchmarks, fuel consumption tracking, unlimited vehicles/photos/reminders, challenges participation, export reports, document storage.

**Phase 2-3 revenue streams:**
- Affiliate partnerships (insurance, tires, parts referrals): commission-based
- Garage subscriptions: €29-49/month
- Dealer subscriptions: €59-99/month
- Fleet subscriptions: €3-5/vehicle/month

**Revenue projections (conservative):**
- Year 1 ARR: ~€10K (Bulgaria B2C only)
- Year 2 ARR: ~€55K-78K (Bulgaria + Romania, B2B start)
- Year 3 ARR: ~€144K-264K (multi-market, full B2B)

### 7. Cost Structure

**Fixed costs (monthly):**

| Cost | Amount | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Cloud hosting (backend + database) | €20-50/month | Tech stack TBD — see technical architecture decision |
| Domain + SSL | ~€2/month | Amortized annual |
| App Store fees (Apple + Google) | €25/year + €8.33/month (amortized) | One-time €25 (Google) + €99/year (Apple) |
| Email/notification service | €0-20/month | Free tier initially |
| Developer tools/subscriptions | €0-30/month | IDE, monitoring, etc. |
| **Total fixed (pre-revenue)** | **~€50-130/month** | |

**Variable costs:**
| Cost | Amount | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| App Store commission | 30% of subscription revenue | Apple/Google take on all IAP |
| Image storage | €0.01-0.03/GB/month | Scales with users + photos |
| Marketing budget (organic phase) | €0-200/month | Car meet costs, small social spend |

**Key cost insights:**
- Solo developer — no salary cost in MVP phase (founder-funded)
- Infrastructure costs are minimal at our scale (<10,000 users)
- The 30% App Store commission is the largest variable cost — factor into unit economics
- Total burn rate pre-revenue: ~€100-300/month (very lean)

### 8. Key Metrics

**North Star Metric:** Weekly active users who log at least 1 expense

| Category | Metric | Target (Month 6) | Target (Month 12) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Acquisition** | Monthly downloads | 800 | 1,500 |
| **Activation** | % who add a vehicle + log 1 expense in first session | 60% | 65% |
| **Retention** | 30-day retention (return and log expense) | 30% | 35% |
| **Revenue** | Premium conversion rate | 6% | 7.5% |
| **Revenue** | Monthly recurring revenue (MRR) | €270 | €810 |
| **Referral** | % of users who share vehicle card or invite friend | 5% | 10% |
| **Engagement** | Expenses logged per active user per week | 1.5 | 2.0 |

**Critical assumption to validate:**
The entire model depends on 30%+ 30-day retention. If users don't come back after week 1, nothing else matters. Retention is driven by:
1. Quick-add UX (low friction to log)
2. Cost dashboard "aha moment" (seeing your real number)
3. Gamification (challenges give reasons to return)
4. Reminders (push notifications for maintenance due dates)

---

### 9. Unfair Advantage

**What can't be easily copied or bought:**

1. **Aggregated cost data from real Bulgarian car owners.** As users log expenses, we build a dataset of real ownership costs per make/model in Bulgaria. This data powers benchmarks and becomes more valuable with every user. Competitors starting later won't have this data.

2. **Community position within Bulgarian car culture.** First-mover advantage in a tight-knit community. If we become "the app" that car enthusiasts use, network effects make it hard for competitors to displace us. Car communities are tribal — once adopted, tools stick.

3. **Local market knowledge.** Built by someone who understands Bulgarian car culture, pricing, pain points, and community dynamics. Global competitors won't localize for a 3.5M-car market.

**Honest assessment:** None of these are absolute moats. A well-funded competitor could replicate features. The defensibility grows over time as data accumulates and community position strengthens. Speed to market and community adoption are critical.

## Assumptions to Validate

| # | Assumption | Risk | Validation Method | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Car enthusiasts want to know their true costs (not just talk about cars) | High | Customer interviews (Q3, Q7, Q13) | Not validated |
| 2 | They will actually log expenses regularly (not just week 1) | High | MVP retention data (30/60/90 day) | Not validated |
| 3 | Manual data entry won't kill retention | High | MVP usage data + quick-add UX testing | Not validated |
| 4 | Cost per kilometer is a compelling "aha moment" | Medium | Interview reactions (Q13) | Not validated |
| 5 | €2.99/month is acceptable in Bulgaria | Medium | Interview pricing questions (Q12) + post-launch conversion | Not validated |
| 6 | Vehicle timeline drives engagement for enthusiasts | Medium | Interviews (Q10) + MVP feature usage | Not validated |
| 7 | Gamification drives repeat usage | Medium | A/B testing post-launch | Not validated |
| 8 | Facebook groups are an effective distribution channel | Medium | Track installs from group posts | Not validated |
| 9 | 7.5% premium conversion is achievable | Medium | Post-launch conversion data | Not validated |
| 10 | Bulgarian market is large enough to validate the concept | Low | Market sizing + launch results | Partially validated (sizing done) |

**Critical path:** Assumptions 1-3 are existential. If people don't want to track, won't log regularly, or find manual entry too painful, the product fails regardless of features. Interviews must probe these hard.
